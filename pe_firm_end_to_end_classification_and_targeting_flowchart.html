1
Firm qualification
4 gates — every PE firm must pass ALL 4 to enter classification
Gate 1
APAC investment mandate?
Does the firm have an office in SG, HK, Mumbai, Jakarta, Sydney, or Tokyo? OR do their portfolio companies have APAC operations/supply chains?
Gate 2
Physical operations sectors?
Is 30%+ of the portfolio in manufacturing, industrials, infrastructure, logistics, energy, consumer goods, healthcare with physical ops, or real assets?
Gate 3
Right size?
AUM $500M–$15B. Team size 20–500. Exception: sovereign wealth funds (GIC, Temasek, INA) bypass this gate entirely.
Gate 4
Active APAC deal flow?
Has the firm closed at least 1 deal involving APAC in the last 24 months? Check portfolio page for recent additions or Google "[firm] Asia acquisition 2024-2026".
All 4 gates passed? → YES: proceed to stage 2  |  Any NO: discard firm
2
Firm classification
7 weighted checks — run in order, each produces a signal, combination determines type
25%
1. Website team page scan
Does a branded operating team page exist with listed members? Navigate to "Our People" → look for separate ops team filter.
Centralized: dedicated page with names
Decentralized: no separate page
20%
2. Language analysis
Does the website say "team of X professionals" or "network of X advisors"? Count centralized vs decentralized phrases.
"team" + "full-time" = centralized
"network" + "advisors" = decentralized
20%
3. LinkedIn headcount test
Search LinkedIn for employees with "operating" / "value creation" titles. Count results and check title patterns.
50+ results with career ladder = centralized
Under 5 results = decentralized
15%
4. Job postings check
Is the firm hiring full-time ops roles? (Associate, VP, Director in ops/value creation)
Ops job postings = centralized
Only deal team roles = decentralized
10%
5. Annual report scan
For public firms only. Does the report name a specific ops team with headcount? Most authoritative source (filed with regulators).
5%
6. Board composition sample
Pick 2-3 portfolio companies. Are ops team members on the board as employees (centralized) or as independent affiliates (decentralized)?
5%
7. Umbrex / third-party cross-reference
Check umbrex.com/resources/private-equity-operating-partner-groups/ for the firm's profile. Confirms or corrects the classification from checks 1-6.
Combine all 7 signals with weights → confidence score (0-100) → if below 60, flag for manual review

5
Title targeting
WHO exactly to find at each level — every level has a different role in the buying decision
Level 1: Associates + analysts
Investment Associate, Analyst, Senior Associate, Investment Analyst
Own the vendor evaluation workflow. They search for advisory firms, request proposals, and recommend to partners. Building relationships here generates traction from the ground up.
Level 2: VPs + directors
Vice President, Director, SVP, Investment Director, Investment Manager
Manage the deal process. Have direct budget authority for $20-50K advisory engagements without needing partner sign-off. Remember which advisors delivered well.
Level 3: Partners + MDs
Partner, Managing Director, Managing Partner, Principal
Decide which deals to pursue and which advisors to bring in. Highest value but hardest to reach. Let Associate/VP relationships create visibility first.
Level 4: Operating team (centralized only)
Operating Partner, VP Value Creation, Director Portfolio Ops, Capstone Associate
Most direct BI buyer at centralized firms. They scope and manage external advisory engagements. One relationship = multiple portfolio company deals.
Level 5: Corporate development at PE-backed portfolio companies (Path B only)
Corp Dev Director, VP Corp Dev, VP Strategy, Head of M&A, CEO, COO, VP Operations, VP Supply Chain, VP International
Sit at the portfolio company. Responsible for growth initiatives including APAC expansion. Buy BI services directly with PE-allocated budget. Highest conversion path.
Outreach sequence: start with Level 1-2 (Associates + VPs) at the PE firm to build ground-up traction → Level 5 (portfolio co operators) for direct pipeline → Level 3-4 (Partners + ops team) once visibility is established
6
Prioritization + outreach sequence
Signal-based scoring determines who gets contacted first
Priority 1 — contact within 48h
Changed jobs in past 30 days + matches ICP title. OR firm just announced an APAC deal. OR portfolio company just posted APAC roles.
Highest intent. Send connection request immediately.
Priority 2 — contact within 1 week
Posted on LinkedIn about APAC in past 14 days. OR company headcount growing 10%+. OR at a firm on your Tier 1 list with no prior outreach.
Strong signal. Engage with content first, then connect.
Priority 3 — nurture cadence
All other ICP matches without active intent signals. Set weekly saved search alerts to catch new entries.
Like/comment their posts for 1-2 weeks before connecting.
Discard — do not contact
No APAC relevance signal. Pure fintech/software PE with no physical ops portfolio. Firm under $500M AUM with no APAC office.
Remove from list. Revisit only if new signals emerge.
Final output: prioritized prospect list in Google Sheet / Apollo / Sales Navigator saved list
Columns: name, title, firm, classification, extraction path, email, LinkedIn, signals, priority score, outreach hook
